The Hike

On the trail

Denver Post photo editor Dean Krakel is spending six weeks hiking the Colorado Trail. He shares a record of his long distance trek on an occasional blog.

A headline for Dean’s first story from the trail

Published July 17, 2015

By Dean Krakel, The Denver Post

Elizabeth Thomas stands on Sentinel Mountain in Olympic National Park. She often hikes alone. Courtesy of Grant Sible

So there I was, standing at a trailhead along U.S. 50 west of Salida last summer, all ready-set-go for a Colorado Trail adventure. I’d taken three weeks off of work to do this. The plan was to hike a 160-mile loop of the Collegiate East and the brand new Collegiate West trail. After that? I’d just see where I ended up. A simple enough plan.

A bold enough plan that I was experiencing some doubt.

That’s mighty big, wild country out there pardner, I was thinking. A lot to take on alone. So I did what any other bold, adventurous hiker would do — I drove to Salida and had another cup of coffee.

I’m no stranger to doing things alone. I’ve skied for a month through Yellowstone National Park, solo biked Utah’s White Rim and kayaked the Green River through Labyrinth Canyon alone. From past experience I know that at the beginning of any adventure, there will be a moment before committing when the “I don’t think this is such a hot idea” voice chimes in.
